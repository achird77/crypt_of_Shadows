## 2. General Image Generation Prompt

Use a **square aspect ratio** (1:1) – suitable for game icons, tokens, or bestiary entries.  
**Recommended resolution:** `1024×1024` pixels (high detail) or `512×512` for faster generation.

### Prompt template (fill in `[MONSTER NAME]` and `[DESCRIPTION]`):

```
Generate a picture.
A dark fantasy illustration of a [MONSTER NAME] from a cursed dungeon crawler game.  
Be creative.  
Style: gritty, painterly, low-angle dramatic lighting, deep shadows, muted earth tones with accent colors (red for blood, green for poison, blue for magic).  
Background: ancient stone walls, faint glowing runes, or darkness.  
The monster should look menacing and ready to fight. No UI, no text, no frame.  
Aspect ratio: 1:1. Resolution: 512x512.
```

### Example for **Goblin**:
> *A dark fantasy illustration of a **Goblin** from a cursed dungeon crawler game. Lean, green-skinned, sharp teeth, ragged leather armor, clutching a rusted shortsword. Sneering and cunning. Style: gritty, painterly, low-angle dramatic lighting, deep shadows, muted earth tones with red accents. Background: ancient stone walls. No UI, no text, no frame. Aspect ratio: 1:1. Resolution: 1024x1024.*

### Example for **The Lich King Aethelgard**:
> *A dark fantasy illustration of **The Lich King Aethelgard** from a cursed dungeon crawler game. Skeletal undead king in tarnished crown and black plate armor, glowing blue eyes, holding a jagged rune blade. Surrounded by swirling shadows. Style: epic, painterly, dramatic lighting. Background: throne room with broken pillars. No UI, no text. 1:1, 1024x1024.*

> **Pro tip:** For bosses, add “epic, towering, imposing” – for regular monsters, add “feral, bestial, agile”. Use consistent style keywords to keep a unified art direction.

## 3. Filename Protocol

Use **lowercase snake_case** with prefixes to separate bosses, regular monsters, and specials. This makes sorting and scripting easy.

### Naming convention:
- **Bosses:** `boss_[short_name].png`  
  *Example:* `boss_grave_warden_aegis.png`, `boss_lich_king.png`
- **Regular monsters:** `monster_[name].png`  
  *Example:* `monster_goblin.png`, `monster_wraith.png`
- **Special monsters:** `special_[name].png`  
  *Example:* `special_mimic.png`, `special_shadow_of_the_lich.png`
